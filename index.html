Glossary

Mortgage & Property Tax Terms

Principal & Interest (P&I)
The monthly loan payment made up of principal (reduces balance) plus interest (cost to borrow).
Millage
A property tax rate expressed in “mills.” 1 mill = $1 per $1,000 of taxable value. Millage rates are subject to change.
Taxable Value (Assumed)
For estimation, taxable value is assumed as 50% of the home price. Actual taxable value is set by the local assessor.
Uncapping (Assumed Possible)
After a sale/transfer, taxable value may “uncap,” which can increase taxes compared to the prior owner’s tax bill. This estimate may not match the post-sale taxable value.
Special Assessments
Special assessments (sewer, water, roads, lake districts, etc.) may apply and can vary by property. These may not be included in millage rates and may differ from this estimate.
Unique Circumstances
Exemptions, abatements, local debt changes, classification details, and other factors can change taxes. Use this as an estimate and confirm with local officials or a professional.
Primary Residence
If it’s a rental or second home, uncheck primary residence so the non-homestead rate applies.
PMI
Private Mortgage Insurance. Often required when putting less than 20% down. Costs vary by lender and borrower profile.
PMMS (Freddie Mac)
Freddie Mac PMMS is a national average rate series used to pre-fill interest rates for 15- and 30-year terms (not a loan offer).
Disclaimer: Estimates only. Property taxes use 2024 Michigan millage rates by county / local unit / school district and PRE vs non-PRE. Uncapping after purchase, millage changes, special assessments, and unique circumstances may cause actual taxes to differ.
